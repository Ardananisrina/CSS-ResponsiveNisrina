Exo is a south Korean-Chinese boy band based Seoul, with nine members: Suho, Xiumin, lay, Baekhyun, Chen, Chanyeol, D.O, Kai, and Sehun. The band was formed by SM Entertainment in 2011 and debuted in 2012. Their music incorporates genres like pop, hiphop, and R&B, alongside electrinic dance music genres like house, trap, and synth-pop. Exo release and performs music in Koren, Mandarin, and Japanese.

The band ranked as one of the top five most influential celebrities on the Forbes Korean Power Celebrity list each year from 2014 to 2018, and have been named "The biggest boy band in the world" and the "kings of K-pop" by the media.Exo has been single group since then, while continuing to release and perform music in multiple languages.

Since 2016, Chen, Baekhyun, and Xiumin have also released music and performed as sub-unit named Exo-CBX. All of EXO's members also maintain solo career in fields including music, film, and television. EXO's firs album XOXO (2013), which contains the breakthrought hit "Growl", was critical and commercial success.
